Research Integrity

Methods, limitations, and accountability.

The standards applied to independent research and public commentary on this site.

Evidence

Claims should be traceable to public records, cited literature, or clearly described secondary analysis. Sources are evaluated for what they support, not merely for institutional prestige.

Uncertainty

Established findings, interpretation, working hypotheses, and unresolved questions should be distinguished wherever practical.

Independence and conflicts

No institutional affiliation is implied by the design or publication record shown on this site. Project-specific funding and material conflicts should be disclosed with the relevant output.

Corrections

Substantive corrections should identify what changed, why it changed, and when. Archived records should be versioned where the publication platform supports it.

Scope

Public commentary is not represented as peer-reviewed research. Preprints, translations, and commentary are labeled separately from journal publications.
